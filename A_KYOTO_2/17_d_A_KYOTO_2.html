22_A_KYOTO_2
What is extracted from the interactions between a master and disciple , or Zen lecture transcripts that record the actions of a master , is called a Koan ( precedent ) and various collections have been compiled since the Song dynasty ; but enlightenment is not something that can be communicated with words , so when people today try to understand the text , the Koan itself refuses them .     [BDS_00002;22]

23_A_KYOTO_2
However , they contain hints that lead people toward enlightenment , and there are a great many Zen books from the beginning down to the present day .     [BDS_00002;23]
