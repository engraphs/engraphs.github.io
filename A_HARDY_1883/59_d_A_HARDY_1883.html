67_A_HARDY_1883
This cessation of activity aroused the solitary pedestrian from the reverie into which he had lapsed , and , emerging from the shed , with an apparently new intention , he walked up the path to the house-door .

68_A_HARDY_1883
Arrived here , his first act was to kneel down on a large stone beside the row of vessels , and to drink a copious draught from one of them .

69_A_HARDY_1883
Having quenched his thirst he rose and lifted his hand to knock , but paused with his eye upon the panel .

70_A_HARDY_1883
Since the dark surface of the wood revealed absolutely nothing , it was evident that he must be mentally looking through the door , as if he wished to measure thereby all the possibilities that a house of this sort might include , and how they might bear upon the question of his entry .

71_A_HARDY_1883
In his indecision he turned and surveyed the scene around .
