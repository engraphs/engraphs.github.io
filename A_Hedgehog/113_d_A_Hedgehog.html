127_A_Hedgehog
The first scientific reference to the hedgehog is in Aristotle ; he is picked up again by Isidore of Seville in the 8th century and again by Buffon in the 18th century , and these are reminders of the ways in which we get hedgehogs wrong .     [2015-11-10;speaker=Stewart]

128_A_Hedgehog
Aristotle points out that the hedgehog carries apples on his spine into his nest .     [2015-11-10;speaker=Stewart]

129_A_Hedgehog
Isidore of Seville argues that the hedgehog travels with grapes embedded on his spine .     [2015-11-10;speaker=Stewart]

130_A_Hedgehog
Buffon believes these things might have been food for the winter , but as we know today the hedgehog , hibernating as he does , is not a creature that needs to take food into his nest for the winter .     [2015-11-10;speaker=Stewart]
