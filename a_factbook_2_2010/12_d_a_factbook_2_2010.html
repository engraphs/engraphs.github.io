50_a_factbook_2_2010
Islamic Law - The most widespread type of religious law , it is the legal system enforced in over 30 countries , particularly in the Near East , but also in Central and South Asia , Africa , and Indonesia .

51_a_factbook_2_2010
In many countries Islamic law operates in tandem with a civil law system .

52_a_factbook_2_2010
Islamic law is embodied in the sharia , an Arabic word meaning the right path .

53_a_factbook_2_2010
Sharia covers all aspects of public and private life and organizes them into five categories : obligatory , recommended , permitted , disliked , and forbidden .

54_a_factbook_2_2010
The primary sources of sharia law are the Qur'an , believed by Muslims to be the word of God revealed to the Prophet Muhammad by the angel Gabriel , and the Sunnah , the teachings of the Prophet and his works .

55_a_factbook_2_2010
In addition to these two primary sources , traditional Sunni Muslims recognize the consensus of Muhammad 's companions and Islamic jurists on certain issues , called ijmas , and various forms of reasoning , including analogy by legal scholars , referred to as qiyas .

56_a_factbook_2_2010
Shia Muslims reject ijmas and qiyas as sources of sharia law .
