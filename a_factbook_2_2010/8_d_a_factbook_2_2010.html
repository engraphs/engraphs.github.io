18_a_factbook_2_2010
Customary Law - A type of legal system that serves as the basis of , or has influenced , the present_-_day laws in approximately 40 countries - mostly in Africa , but some in the Pacific islands , Europe , and the Near East .

19_a_factbook_2_2010
Customary law is also referred to as primitive law , unwritten law , indigenous law , and folk law .

20_a_factbook_2_2010
There is no single history of customary law such as that found in Roman civil law , English common law , Islamic law , or the Napoleonic Civil Code .

21_a_factbook_2_2010
The earliest systems of law in human society were customary , and usually developed in small agrarian and hunter_-_gatherer communities .

22_a_factbook_2_2010
As the term implies , customary law is based upon the customs of a community .

23_a_factbook_2_2010
Common attributes of customary legal systems are that they are seldom written down , they embody an organized set of rules regulating social relations , and they are agreed upon by members of the community .

24_a_factbook_2_2010
Although such law systems include sanctions for law infractions , resolution tends to be reconciliatory rather than punitive .

25_a_factbook_2_2010
A number of African states practiced customary law many centuries prior to colonial influences .

26_a_factbook_2_2010
Following colonization , such laws were written down and incorporated to varying extents into the legal systems imposed by their colonial powers .
