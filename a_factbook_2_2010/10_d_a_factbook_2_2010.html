32_a_factbook_2_2010
French Law - A type of civil law that is the legal system of France .

33_a_factbook_2_2010
The French system also serves as the basis for , or is mixed with , other legal systems in approximately 50 countries , notably in North Africa , the Near East , and the French territories and dependencies .

34_a_factbook_2_2010
French law is primarily codified or systematic written civil law .

35_a_factbook_2_2010
Prior to the French Revolution ( 1789_-_1799 ) , France had no single national legal system .

36_a_factbook_2_2010
Laws in the northern areas of present_-_day France were mostly local customs based on privileges and exemptions granted by kings and feudal lords , while in the southern areas Roman law predominated .

37_a_factbook_2_2010
The introduction of the Napoleonic Civil Code during the reign of Napoleon I in the first decade of the 19th century brought major reforms to the French legal system , many of which remain part of France 's current legal structure , though all have been extensively amended or redrafted to address a modern nation .

38_a_factbook_2_2010
French law distinguishes between public law and private law .

39_a_factbook_2_2010
Public law relates to government , the French Constitution , public administration , and criminal law .

40_a_factbook_2_2010
Private law covers issues between private citizens or corporations .

41_a_factbook_2_2010
The most recent changes to the French legal system - introduced in the 1980s - were the decentralization laws , which transferred authority from centrally appointed government representatives to locally elected representatives of the people .
