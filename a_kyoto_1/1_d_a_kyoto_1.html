1_a_kyoto_1
Sesshu     [BDS_00001;1]

2_a_kyoto_1
Known as Sesshu ( 1420_-_1506 ) , he was an ink painter and Zen monk active in the Muromachi period in the latter half of the 15th century , and was called a master painter .     [BDS_00001;2]

3_a_kyoto_1
He revolutionized the Japanese ink painting .     [BDS_00001;3]

4_a_kyoto_1
He was given the posthumous name “ Toyo ” or “ Sesshu ( 拙宗 ) . ”     [BDS_00001;4]

5_a_kyoto_1
Born in Bicchu Province , he moved to Suo Province after entering Shokoku_-_ji Temple in Kyoto .     [BDS_00001;5]

6_a_kyoto_1
Later he accompanied a mission to Ming Dynasty China and learned Chinese ink painting .     [BDS_00001;6]

7_a_kyoto_1
His works were many , including not only Chinese_-_style landscape paintings , but also portraits and pictures of flowers and birds .     [BDS_00001;7]

8_a_kyoto_1
His bold compositions and strong brush strokes constituted an extremely distinctive style .     [BDS_00001;8]

9_a_kyoto_1
6 of his extant works are designated national treasures . Indeed , he is considered to be extraordinary among Japanese painters .     [BDS_00001;9]

10_a_kyoto_1
For this reason , there are a great many artworks that are attributed to him , such as folding screens with pictures of flowers and that birds are painted on them .     [BDS_00001;10]

11_a_kyoto_1
There are many works that even experts can not agree if they are really his work or not .     [BDS_00001;11]
