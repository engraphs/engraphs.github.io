1The Prime Minister's letter will be sent to 30 million households, Number 10 said.
2Read it in full here.
3I am writing to you to update you on the steps we are taking to combat coronavirus.
4In just a few short weeks, everyday life in this country has changed dramatically.
5We all feel the profound impact of coronavirus not just on ourselves, but on our loved ones and our communities.
6I understand completely the difficulties this disruption has caused to your lives, businesses and jobs.
7But the action we have taken is absolutely necessary, for one very simple reason.
8If too many people become seriously unwell at one time, the NHS will be unable to cope.
9This will cost lives.
10We must slow the spread of the disease, and reduce the number of people needing hospital treatment in order to save as many lives as possible.
11That is why we are giving one simple instruction - you must stay at home.
12You should not meet friends or relatives who do not live in your home.
13You may only leave your home for very limited purposes, such as buying food and medicine, exercising once a day and seeking medical attention.
14You can travel to and from work but should work from home if you can.
15When you do have to leave your home, you should ensure, wherever possible, that you are two metres apart from anyone outside of your household.
16These rules must be observed.
17So, if people break the rules, the police will issue fines and disperse gatherings.
18I know many of you will be deeply worried about the financial impact on you and your family.
19The government will do whatever it takes to help you make ends meet and put food on the table.
20The enclosed leaflet sets out more detail about the support available and the rules you need to follow.
21You can also find the latest advice at gov.uk / coronavirus
22From the start, we have sought to put in the right measures at the right time.
23We will not hesitate to go further if that is what the scientific and medical advice tells us we must do.
24It's important for me to level with you - we know things will get worse before they get better.
25But we are making the right preparations, and the more we all follow the rules, the fewer lives will be lost and the sooner life can return to normal.
26I want to thank everyone who is working flat out to beat the virus, in particular the staff in our fantastic NHS and care sector across England, Scotland, Wales and Northern Ireland.
27It has been truly inspirational to see our doctors, nurses and other carers rise magnificently to the needs of the hour.
28Thousands of retired doctors and nurses are returning to the NHS - and hundreds of thousands of citizens are volunteering to help the most vulnerable.
29It is with that great British spirit that we will beat coronavirus and we will beat it together.
30That is why, at this moment of national emergency, I urge you, please, to stay at home, protect the NHS and save lives.
31Boris Johnson
