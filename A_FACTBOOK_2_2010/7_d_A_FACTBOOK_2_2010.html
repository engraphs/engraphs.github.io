11_A_FACTBOOK_2_2010
Common Law - A type of legal system , often synonymous with English common law , which is the system of England and Wales in the UK , and is also in force in approximately 80 countries formerly part of or influenced by the former British Empire .

12_A_FACTBOOK_2_2010
English common law reflects Biblical influences as well as remnants of law systems imposed by early conquerors including the Romans , Anglo-Saxons , and Normans .

13_A_FACTBOOK_2_2010
Some legal scholars attribute the formation of the English common law system to King Henry II ( r.1154-1189 ) .

14_A_FACTBOOK_2_2010
Until the time of his reign , laws customary among England 's various manorial and ecclesiastical ( church ) jurisdictions were administered locally .

15_A_FACTBOOK_2_2010
Henry II established the king 's court and designated that laws were common to the entire English realm .

16_A_FACTBOOK_2_2010
The foundation of English common law is legal precedent - referred to as stare decisis , meaning to stand by things decided .

17_A_FACTBOOK_2_2010
In the English common law system , court judges are bound in their decisions in large part by the rules and other doctrines developed - and supplemented over time - by the judges of earlier English courts .
