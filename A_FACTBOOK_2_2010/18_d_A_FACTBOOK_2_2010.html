81_A_FACTBOOK_2_2010
Roman-Dutch Law - A type of civil law based on Roman law as applied in the Netherlands .

82_A_FACTBOOK_2_2010
Roman-Dutch law serves as the basis for legal systems in seven African countries , as well as Guyana , Indonesia , and Sri Lanka .

83_A_FACTBOOK_2_2010
This law system , which originated in the province of Holland and expanded throughout the Netherlands ( to be replaced by the French Civil Code in 1809 ) , was instituted in a number of sub-Saharan African countries during the Dutch colonial period .

84_A_FACTBOOK_2_2010
The Dutch jurist / philosopher Hugo Grotius was the first to attempt to reduce Roman-Dutch civil law into a system in his Jurisprudence of Holland ( written 1619-20 , commentary published 1621 ) .

85_A_FACTBOOK_2_2010
The Dutch historian / lawyer Simon van Leeuwen coined the term Roman-Dutch law in 1652 .
