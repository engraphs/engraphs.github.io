97_A_FACTBOOK_2_2010
United States Law - A type of common law , which is the basis of the legal system of the United States and that of its island possessions in the Caribbean and the Pacific .

98_A_FACTBOOK_2_2010
This legal system has several layers , more possibly than in most other countries , and is due in part to the division between federal and state law .

99_A_FACTBOOK_2_2010
The United States was founded not as one nation but as a union of 13 colonies , each claiming independence from the British Crown .

100_A_FACTBOOK_2_2010
The US Constitution , implemented in 1789 , began shifting power away from the states and toward the federal government , though the states today retain substantial legal authority .

101_A_FACTBOOK_2_2010
US law draws its authority from four sources : constitutional law , statutory law , administrative regulations , and case law .

102_A_FACTBOOK_2_2010
Constitutional law is based on the US Constitution and serves as the supreme federal law .

103_A_FACTBOOK_2_2010
Taken together with those of the state constitutions , these documents outline the general structure of the federal and state governments and provide the rules and limits of power .

104_A_FACTBOOK_2_2010
US statutory law is legislation enacted by the US Congress and is codified in the United States Code .

105_A_FACTBOOK_2_2010
The 50 state legislatures have similar authority to enact state statutes .

106_A_FACTBOOK_2_2010
Administrative law is the authority delegated to federal and state executive agencies .

107_A_FACTBOOK_2_2010
Case law , also referred to as common law , covers areas where constitutional or statutory law is lacking .

108_A_FACTBOOK_2_2010
Case law is a collection of judicial decisions , customs , and general principles that began in England centuries ago , that were adopted in America at the time of the Revolution , and that continue to develop today .
